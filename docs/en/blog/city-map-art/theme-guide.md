# The MapVibe Theme Guide: Which Visual Style Is Yours?

Sixty-two themes is a lot of themes.

If you've opened the MapVibe editor and immediately felt paralysed by the dropdown, you're not alone. The purpose of this guide is simple: describe each major theme category in terms of what it *feels like*, so you can go in already knowing the direction.

You don't need to try all 62. You need to try three.

---

## Why Theme Matters More Than You Think

The same neighbourhood looks like a completely different city depending on the theme.

Mediterranean Vibes makes Caracas feel like a coastal capital baking in afternoon light. Vintage Noir makes it look like the setting of a 1960s political thriller. Neon Night makes it electric and contemporary. Big Apple Energy makes it feel dense and purposeful.

Same streets. Same bearing. Same zoom. Four completely different prints.

## The Major Themes — In Plain Language

### Mediterranean Vibes
**Warm ivory background, terracotta-to-gold colour palette, soft blue water tones.**

This is the theme for cities that have a relationship with the sun. It works beautifully on coastal cities — Cartagena, Lisbon, Valencia, Havana — but it also works on inland cities that carry warmth in the memory: Caracas, Medellín, Guadalajara.

If you want the map to feel like afternoon light through old curtains, this is it.

![Caracas Chacao — Mediterranean Vibes, 30° bearing](/assets/images/theme-guide_caracas-med-vibes-30deg.png)

*Caracas Chacao district in Mediterranean Vibes at 30°. The colour palette echoes the city's warm, canyon-lit quality.*

---

### Vintage Noir
**Near-black background, cream street lines, monochrome water fills, high contrast.**

Vintage Noir is cinematic. It makes every city look like a location scout photo from a film set in a city that never quite existed.

This theme works everywhere — but it's particularly powerful on cities with geometric density: São Paulo's central grid, Buenos Aires' barrios, Bogotá's expanded downtown. The high contrast makes every street visible. The dark ground makes the geometry feel intentional rather than inherited.

![Caracas Chacao — Vintage Noir, 30° bearing](/assets/images/theme-guide_caracas-vintage-noir-30deg.png)

*The same Caracas neighbourhood in Vintage Noir. Cinematic, high-contrast — completely different emotional register from Mediterranean Vibes.*

---

### Neon Night
**Near-black background, electric accent colours (teal, magenta, or cyan), vivid water fills.**

For cities that define themselves by their nightlife or urban energy: Mexico City's Roma Norte, Bogotá's Chapinero, São Paulo's Vila Madalena. Neon Night amplifies the feeling that the city is awake.

This theme reads well in rooms with dark walls and artificial lighting. It's less suited to bright, airy spaces.

---

### Warm Beige
**Cream-to-tan base, muted greens, warm-toned water. Softer than Mediterranean Vibes, less contrast.**

The "safe" warm option. Works in almost any interior. Reads as elegant without being bold. Good choice when the map is a gift and you don't know the recipient's taste — it will pair with most furniture.

---

### Big Apple Energy
**High saturation, bold block colours, geometric fill emphasis. Inspired by mid-century New York cartographic design.**

This is the theme for people who want the map to be decoration in the most deliberate sense — a design object, not just a map. It pulls attention. It works best in spaces designed to hold strong visual statements.

---

### Galactic Forge
**Deep space-blue background, luminous street fills, sharp contrast. Futuristic.**

The furthest from "classic map aesthetic." Galactic Forge treats the city as a circuit board — abstract, geometric, striking. If you want to give someone a map of their city that they initially don't recognise as a map, this is the theme.

---

### Neon Shores
**Dark coastal blue background, warm-amber street fills, strong coast/water contrast.**

Specifically designed to make coastal cities look electric and cinematic from the water's edge. Works especially well when the zoom level is set to include a significant water body — a bay, a river bend, a harbour.

## How to Run the Speedrun

The best way to find your theme is to:

1. Set your neighbourhood, bearing, and zoom first
2. Open the theme dropdown
3. Switch through each one for 5 seconds

You'll know within the first 3 themes whether you're in warm-palette or dark-palette territory. Within 6 themes, you'll have a finalist.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I change the theme after ordering?**
Not after the order is placed. The print is rendered to your exact specifications. Use the editor to experiment freely before checkout — there's no limit on how many times you switch.

**Which theme is most popular?**
Mediterranean Vibes and Vintage Noir are the two most ordered. Warm Beige is the most common "I'm not sure, play it safe" choice. Galactic Forge is the most polarising — people who love it, love it completely.

**Do themes change the file resolution or print quality?**
No. All themes are rendered at the same resolution. The theme affects colour and visual style only.

**I want something that isn't in the 62 themes — can you do custom?**
Not currently. The 62 themes cover most major visual registers, and the combination with bearing, zoom, and neighbourhood creates enough variation that most people find their exact look within the editor.

## The Prints That Resonate for Colombian Expats

**Bogotá**
At neighbourhood level, Bogotá reveals itself differently than from a distance. La Candelaria's colonial grid, Chapinero's diagonal streets, Usaquén's residential texture — zoomed to barrio scale at a 30–45° bearing, Bogotá becomes graphic art rather than city overview. The altitude doesn't come through in maps, but the density does.

**Medellín**
The city in the valley. El Poblado's hillside curves, Laureles' linear avenues, the metro line threading through the centre — Medellín's topography is one of the most interesting cartographic subjects in Latin America. At medium zoom, the bowl shape created by the Andes framing the city comes through clearly. Vintage Noir works particularly well for Medellín's urban density.

**Cartagena**
The walled city is one of the most cartographically perfect subjects anywhere. The medieval street layout inside the walls, the Getsemaní neighbourhood just outside, the peninsula shape — at the right zoom, a Cartagena print is unmistakably Colombian and unlike anything else. Mediterranean Vibes for the warmth; the colours suit the Caribbean.

**Cali, Barranquilla, Bucaramanga**
Each carries its own cartographic character. Cali's river-crossing grid. Barranquilla's Caribbean port geometry. Bucaramanga's canyon-edge urbanity. If you know someone from any of these — the map exists. We just need to centre it on *their* street.

**The Pueblo**
The town in the eje cafetero, the *municipio* in Boyacá, the village in the Llanos that appears only on regional maps. The central plaza, the church, the streets that radiate outward. MapVibe prints it. Probably no one else does.

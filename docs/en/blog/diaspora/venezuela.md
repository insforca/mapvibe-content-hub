# Venezuelan Diaspora: The Map of the City You Carry

Wherever you are — Madrid, Miami, Bogotá, Santiago, Toronto — you carry Venezuela with you.

Not the headlines. The place. The *barrio*. The corner of Caracas where you grew up. The *pueblo* in the Andes or the Llanos that nobody outside Venezuela has ever thought to map.

MapVibe makes that print real.

---

## The Cities and Places That Matter

**Caracas**
Nestled in a valley at 900 metres, Caracas is cartographically unusual — a city compressed by mountains on both sides, with a density that becomes graphic art at neighbourhood zoom. Chacao, Altamira, El Hatillo, El Paraíso. At a diagonal bearing, the valley's east-west compression becomes visible. Try Vintage Noir for urban Caracas; Mediterranean Vibes for the eastern municipalities.

**Maracaibo**
Venezuela's second city, built around the lake. The relationship between the urban grid and the water's edge creates striking prints at medium zoom.

**Mérida**
Andean city in a narrow valley — one of the most geographically dramatic urban layouts in South America. Streets forced to follow the valley floor, the mountains implied by the density of contour. A reminder of place in every sense.

**Valencia, Barquisimeto, Puerto La Cruz**
Each with its own character. Each worthy of a print. Each unmapped by every art store that's never thought past the five usual cities.

**The Pueblo**
The town in Táchira, Lara, Sucre, Bolívar, Barinas. The one with the colonial plaza and the church and the grid that has been there for three hundred years. MapVibe prints it. You only have to find it on the map.

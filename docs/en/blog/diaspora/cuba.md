## The Prints That Resonate for Cuban Expats

**La Habana**
At neighbourhood level, Havana is unlike any other city in the Americas. Vedado's geometric grid broken by diagonal avenues. Centro Habana's compressed colonial density. Miramar's wide mansion-lined streets. Old Havana's layered centuries of urban form. Each neighbourhood has its own map — its own texture and story. At a 30° bearing, Vedado's diagonal street logic becomes visible in a completely different way.

**Trinidad**
Trinidad is one of the most perfectly preserved colonial grids in the Caribbean. The cobblestone streets, the central plaza, the way the town sits at the foot of the Escambray — at medium zoom, a Trinidad map captures five centuries of urban form in a single frame. Few maps anywhere are as immediately recognizable to those who know the city.

**Santiago de Cuba**
Cuba's second city has the topography of its character. Streets that climb and fall, the Cobre road, the bay below, the Oriente geography that shaped the city's history. Santiago doesn't map like Havana — the terrain forces a different urban logic, and at neighbourhood level that difference becomes striking. Vintage Noir suits the city's intensity.

**Cienfuegos**
Cuba's most classically European-planned city, laid out in the 19th century with a rare precision. The grid is almost architectural — the Paseo del Prado, the bay on three sides, the Punta Gorda peninsula. A Cienfuegos print at medium zoom looks like a design document for a city that was drawn before it was built.

**Varadero**
The peninsula geography — one road, the sea on both sides, the beach resort strip — makes Varadero one of the most distinctive map shapes in Cuba. For someone whose memories are tied to the peninsula rather than the city, a Varadero map reads differently than a tourist postcard: it's geography that holds time.

**The Pueblo**
The town in Camagüey, the *municipio* in Holguín, the village in Pinar del Río. The central park, the church, the *calle principal* with the same bodegas. MapVibe prints it. The map exists even when the place feels very far away.

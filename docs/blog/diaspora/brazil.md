Whether it's Vila Madalena, Pinheiros, Santa Teresa, Lapa — or the *cidade* in Minas Gerais, the town in Bahia, the village in the interior that no art store has ever heard of — MapVibe can print it.

## The Prints That Resonate for Brazilian Expats

**São Paulo**
The city that never stops. At neighbourhood level — Pinheiros, Vila Madalena, Consolação, Bixiga — the density and texture of São Paulo's streets becomes graphic art. At a 30–45° bearing, the diagonal pattern of the *avenidas* cutting through the *bairros* creates something genuinely striking.

<!-- IMAGE: São Paulo / Vila Madalena at 30°, Mediterranean Vibes, medium neighbourhood zoom -->

**Rio de Janeiro**
Rio's topography — mountains meeting coast, beaches arcing between peaks — is one of the most visually dramatic maps anywhere. Santa Teresa, Lapa, and the Centro neighbourhoods at medium zoom show a city shaped by geography in a way flat cities never can. Vintage Noir works particularly well for Rio.

**Belo Horizonte, Florianópolis, Salvador, Recife**
Each has its own cartographic character. BH's modernist planned grid. Florianópolis' island geography. Salvador's peninsula. Recife's rivers and bridges. If you know someone from any of these — or any *cidade* in between — there's a print waiting.

**The Village**
The town in Minas, Goiás, Bahia, Rio Grande do Sul that no one outside Brazil has ever heard of. The one with the central *praça* and the church and the grid of streets laid down centuries ago. MapVibe prints it. Probably no one else will.

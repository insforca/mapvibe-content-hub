# Perspective Shift

Every map store sells the same thing: the city from above, facing north, centered on the famous landmark.

**MapVibe starts where that ends.**

A perspective shift means choosing *your* view. The block you grew up on as the center of the world. The neighbourhood that's yours — not the skyline on every tourist poster. The city as *you* know it, not as the guidebook presents it.

---

## Why Standard Maps Miss the Point

When you're abroad and you think about home, you don't think about the skyline.  
You think about a specific corner. A specific street. The way the neighbourhood looked when you walked home.

That's what a Perspective Shift map captures.

::: tabs
**Standard Art Store**
- Center: Famous landmark
- Bearing: 0° north-up
- Zoom: Whole city
- Result: Generic. Same as everyone else's.
---
**MapVibe Perspective Shift**
- Center: Your block, your neighbourhood
- Bearing: Any angle you choose
- Zoom: The streets you actually remember
- Result: Yours. Only yours.
:::

---

## How to Create Your Perspective Shift

1. Open [app.mapvibestudio.com](https://app.mapvibestudio.com)
2. Search your city, then **scroll and drag to your exact neighbourhood**
3. Adjust **zoom** until the streets you remember fill the frame
4. Try different **bearings** — sometimes 30° or 45° reveals a completely different feel
5. Pick the theme that matches the mood of your memory

::: tip
The perspective shift is most powerful for expats: it turns a general city map into a personal memory. Your friends will ask "what city is that?" — and you'll say "my street."
:::

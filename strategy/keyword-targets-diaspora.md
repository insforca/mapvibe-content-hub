> **Q: Which cities have the most beautiful street grid patterns for map art?**
> Savannah (GA) with its 22 historic squares, Chicago at a diagonal bearing showing the lakefront grid, Washington DC's radial avenues over a rectilinear grid, and Barcelona's Eixample with chamfered octagonal blocks are among the most visually striking. Colonial Latin American cities with central plaza grids also produce exceptional map prints.

> **Q: What are geometric street map posters?**
> Geometric street map posters are custom map prints that highlight the mathematical patterns in a city's street layout — grids, radial plans, river bends, and plaza structures — often enhanced by choosing a non-standard bearing or tight zoom level that makes the geometry visible.

> **Q: What does a city look like as a map poster from above?**
> Depending on zoom, bearing, and theme, a city map poster can look like abstract art. Grid cities like Chicago or Savannah at 30–45° bearing, or colonial town plazas zoomed in, produce geometric patterns that stop conversations on a wall.
